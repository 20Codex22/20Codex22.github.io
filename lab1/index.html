How to Meal Plan

By Nora V

1. Decide on what type of meal you want to plan.

Select just one meal type, like lunch, dinner, or breakfast. Weekdays are the busiest for most of us, so we’ll plan for breakfasts in this example.

2. Get inspired through research.

Look at Pinterest, cookbooks, or social media to get ideas for what you might want to cook. When starting out, keep the recipies simple (5 ingredients or less) and the flavors familiar. Save the Recipies.



This website has alot of breakfast ideas to get you started.

3. Make a shopping list.

Make a list with colums for items you Have on Hand, Protein, Veg/Fruit, Carbs, and Other. Look in your pantry for dry ingredients you already have. These might include dry carbs like pasta, rices, beans, or spices. Don’t forget to look for storage containers and aluminum foil to store the meals. Write that in the ‘Have on Hand’ Column. Identify the protein you’ll need (meat, egg, or alternatives) Write the item in the coordinating column on your list. Identify fresh veggies you’ll need to complete the shopping list.

Tip: Looks through online grocery store flyer for budget friendly ingredients. Then swap out that item, for examle, ham for bacon or tofu.

4. Now do some Math.

In this example, we’ll make egg/ham/potato breakfast bowls. My recipe for one bowl calls for:

  • 2 eggs
  • 2 oz ham
  • 1/4 small onion
  • 1 small pototoe
  • sprinkle of shredded cheese
  • 1/2 tsp taco seasoning
  • salt and pepper to taste

For five bowls, I’ll need 10 eggs, 10 oz ham, 1.5 small onions, and 5 potatoes. Upon examining my pantry and fridge, it looks like I’ll need just eggs and ham.

Let's go shopping!

5. Lets cook!

Now we cook! Follow the recipe to cook the meal, and portion it into 5 storage containers. Leave enough time to let everything cool completely before covering and storing in the fridge.

You just meal prepper breakfast for the week. Go you!

Tip: Meal planning is great, but some weeks just have other plans. A good tip is to make just one extra meal and pop that one in the freezer. That way, you can start building up a freezer meal stash too!
