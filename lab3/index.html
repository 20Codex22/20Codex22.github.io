Meal plan with me.

Nora V

Feeding yourself well is one of the mainstays of good self care. This can be daunting if you wait to be hungry or inspired by a recipe to begin cooking. Meal prepping or bulk cooking is one way to ensure we eat well and have a delicious and nutricious meal waiting for us, and not the other way around.

Here is one way to meal prep, in 5 easy steps.

Step 1. Decide on what type of meal you want to plan.

Select just one meal type, like lunch, dinner, or breakfast. Weekdays are the busiest for most of us, so we’ll plan for breakfasts in this example.

Step 2. Get inspired through research.

Look at Pinterest, cookbooks, or social media to get ideas for what you might want to cook. When starting out, keep the recipies simple (5 ingredients or less) and the flavors familiar. Save the Recipies.

Step 3. Make a shopping list.

Make a list with colums for items you Have on Hand, Protein, Veg/Fruit, Carbs, and Other. Look in your pantry for dry ingredients you already have. These might include dry carbs like pasta, rices, beans, or spices. Don’t forget to look for storage containers and aluminum foil too!

Write what you have on hand in the ‘Have’ Column. Identify the protein you’ll need (meat, egg, or alternatives) and add to your list. Do the same with veggies and fruit to complete the shopping list.

soup_kitchen Tip: Looks through online grocery store flyer for sales on budget friendly ingredients. Then swap out that item in your recipe.

Step 4. Now do some Math.

In this example, we’ll make egg/ham/potato breakfast bowls. The recipe for one bowl calls for:

  • 2 eggs
  • 2 oz ham
  • 1/4 small onion
  • 1 small potatoe
  • sprinkle of shredded cheese
  • 1 tsp taco seasoning
  • salt and pepper to taste

For five bowls, I’ll need 10 eggs, 10 oz ham, 1.5 small onions, and 5 potatoes. Upon examining my pantry and fridge, it looks like I’ll need just eggs and ham.

Let's go shopping!

Step 5. Lets cook!

Now we cook! Follow the recipe to cook the meal, and portion it into 5 storage containers. Leave enough time to let everything cool completely before covering and storing in the fridge.

You just meal prepper breakfast for the week. Go you!

soup_kitchen Tip: Meal planning is great, but some weeks just have other plans. A good tip is to make just one extra meal and pop that one in the freezer. That way, you can start building up a freezer meal stash too!
